Introduction

The INDICATE Data Dictionary is a web application for exploring and aligning clinical data with the INDICATE Data Dictionary — a consensus-based collection of standardized clinical concepts designed to harmonize intensive care unit (ICU) data across Europe.

What is INDICATE?

INDICATE is a European initiative launched in December 2024, co-funded by the European Union's Digital Europe Programme (Grant Agreement No. 101167778). The project aims to develop a federated infrastructure for secure, cross-border ICU data sharing, enabling data-driven clinical decision-making and the development of trustworthy AI models for intensive care.

Why a Data Dictionary?

Even when using standardized terminologies like LOINC and SNOMED CT, a single clinical concept can be represented by hundreds of distinct codes, varying by measurement method, specimen type, or clinical context. Without expert guidance, selecting the most appropriate standard concept for each local variable becomes a complex and error-prone task.

The INDICATE Data Dictionary addresses this challenge by providing explicit recommendations to harmonize terminology selection across diverse European ICU settings. It is designed to be agnostic of the ETL model, supporting both OMOP CDM and FHIR-based transformations.

General Concepts Approach

The dictionary is organized around general concepts — clinically meaningful variables as they appear in study protocols. When designing a clinical study, researchers refer to "Heart Rate" or "Serum Creatinine" rather than specific LOINC codes with their method and specimen variations. Our dictionary adopts this pragmatic level of definition, grouping related terminology codes under intuitive clinical concepts organized by categories (vital signs, laboratory, medications, etc.).

Each general concept can be expressed as a concept set compatible with OHDSI ATLAS, combining multiple standard codes with inclusion/exclusion logic and descendant handling. This interoperability with the ATLAS concept set format increases the relevance of the dictionary for observational research and cohort definition.

Key Features

Dictionary Explorer

Browse standardized clinical concepts organized by category: demographics, conditions, vital signs, laboratory, ventilation, drugs, and more.

Concept Alignment

Align your local source concepts with standard terminology concepts to ensure semantic harmonization.

Standard Terminologies

Each concept is mapped to standard terminologies (SNOMED CT, LOINC, RxNorm), with links to ATHENA and the FHIR Terminology Server.

Expert Comments

Access guidance from domain experts — clinicians, biologists, medical informatics specialists, and data scientists — to help with concept selection during ETL. Continuously enriched.

Projects

Organize concepts into projects corresponding to specific clinical use cases or research requirements.

OHDSI Interoperability

Export alignments in formats compatible with OHDSI tools: Usagi, SOURCE_TO_CONCEPT_MAP table, and ATLAS concept sets.

LLM-Assisted Concept Mapping

The application supports concept mapping through CLI tools such as Claude Code or Mistral Vibe CLI, with the option to use local models (e.g. via Ollama). The combination of expert comments and statistical similarity (based on value distributions) significantly improves the accuracy of automated alignments.

About the INDICATE Project

The INDICATE consortium comprises partners from 12 European countries, with 15 data providers implementing a pan-European federated infrastructure. The data dictionary development was driven by six clinical use cases.

Regular interdisciplinary meetings brought together clinical experts, data scientists, and interoperability specialists to map clinical requirements to standard terminologies. The collaborative methodology proved essential for aligning clinical priorities with technical feasibility.

Learn more at indicate-europe.eu.
